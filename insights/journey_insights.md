# Insights – International Student Journey Analytics

## 1. Orientation is a critical conversion point

A noticeable drop-off occurs between arrival and orientation attendance.

**Insight:**
Students who do not attend orientation are significantly less likely to engage and be retained.

**Recommendation:**
- Make orientation mandatory or highly incentivized
- Provide flexible timing (online + offline)
- Send reminders before arrival

---

## 2. Engagement strongly correlates with retention

Students who participate in events (event_participation > 0) show much higher retention.

**Insight:**
Engagement is not optional — it directly impacts student success.

**Recommendation:**
- Increase number of student events
- Personalize event recommendations
- Track low-engagement students early

---

## 3. Early-stage drop-offs (Visa → Arrival)

Some students complete visa but never arrive.

**Insight:**
There is a gap between visa approval and actual campus arrival.

**Recommendation:**
- Introduce pre-arrival engagement programs
- Offer relocation support and guidance
- Provide airport pickup / onboarding assistance

---

## 4. Check-in completion impacts downstream journey

Students who do not complete check-in tend to drop off later.

**Insight:**
Operational steps affect long-term engagement.

**Recommendation:**
- Simplify check-in process
- Provide step-by-step guidance
- Add digital check-in options

---

## 5. Funnel visibility is key

Tracking the full funnel helps identify bottlenecks.

**Insight:**
Without stage-level tracking, universities cannot identify where students are lost.

**Recommendation:**
- Build dashboards for each journey stage
- Monitor weekly drop-offs
- Assign ownership for each stage

---

## Final Takeaway

Improving student journey is not just operational — it is strategic.

Focusing on:
- Orientation
- Engagement
- Early onboarding

can significantly improve retention outcomes.
